1. Category & Market Insights

The"Tangible" Advantage: Backers are attracted to media they can touch.

Physical Dominance: In Tech and Design, almost all funding goes to Hardware and Product Design

The"Hub" Effect: Smaller, high-trust creative hubs like Edinburgh outperform global centers like Brooklyn.

2. Performance Trends & Comparison

The Funding Gap:"Tech and Design" creators meet their goals while "Publishing and Fashion" creators raise only a tiny fraction of their ask.

Launch Day Dead-Zone: It is best to launch Mid-week, avoid weekends.

The MagnitudeThe of Viral Success: Success stories (like Oculus) don't just "hit" goals; they blow past them by millions.

3. Flow & Distribution

Low-Cost, Mass Appeal: This strategy has a very high success rate.

Buy-in Friction: Successful projects keep their entry-level rewards under $200.

Multi-Factor Success Paths: A video is a "must-have," but Facebook presence is the ultimate "deal-maker.

4. Success Drivers & Indicators

The "Danger Zone": Goals under $10,000 fail most often around the 30-day mark.

Backer Unit Value: Tech backers spend the most, whereas Games and Comics require a larger volume due to lower average spend.

The Social Proof Paradox: A Facebook presence is critical but you must market to strangers to succeed even with a high friend count.
