1. Category & Market Insights

The"Tangible" Advantage: Backers are attracted to media they can touch.

Physical Dominance: In Tech and Design, almost all funding goes to Hardware and Product Design

The"Hub" Effect: Smaller, high-trust creative hubs like Edinburgh outperform global centers like Brooklyn.

2. Performance Trends & Comparison

The Funding Gap:"Tech and Design" creators meet their goals while "Publishing and Fashion" creators raise only a tiny fraction of their ask.

Launch Day Dead-Zone: It is best to launch Mid-week, avoid weekends.

The MagnitudeThe of Viral Success: Success stories (like Oculus) don't just "hit" goals; they blow past them by millions.

3. Flow & Distribution

Low-Cost Mass Appeal: "The Crowd" strategy (low cost, mass appeal) has a 93.7% success rate, proving you don't need high-ticket items to succeed if your volume is high.

Buy-in Friction: Successful projects keep their entry-level rewards under $200. Failed projects often attempt to set their lowest "buy-in" far too high, killing momentum.

Niche Flow: Creative "niche" categories like Dance, Theater, and Comics have a much smoother flow toward success than broad, high-competition categories like Technology.

4. Success Drivers & Indicators

The "Danger Zone": The highest concentration of failures occurs at the 30-day mark for goals under $10,000. This is where lack of preparation meets the reality of the platform.

Backer Unit Value: Technology backers spend the most per person ($140), whereas Games and Comics require a much larger "volume" of people to reach the same goal due to lower average spends ($45).

The Social Proof Paradox: A Facebook presence is required for legitimacy, but a high Facebook friend count has a very low correlation (0.07) with actually winning, meaning you must market to strangers to succeed.
